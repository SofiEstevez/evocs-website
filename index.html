One program. Three engines working together.

Most organisations manage identity, licences, and assets in isolation. The result is delayed onboarding, stale access, over-spend on software, and hardware that disappears when people leave.

BARI replaces all of that with a single, automated platform. Workday drives every decision. Every lifecycle event triggers the right action across every connected system — instantly, with a full audit trail and no manual steps.

The problem

Manual identity management is a liability.

When access is managed by hand, security gaps, wasted spend, and compliance risk are inevitable.

New hires wait days for access

Without automation, IT must manually provision accounts across every system. New employees spend their first days unable to do their job while waiting for tickets to be resolved.

Leavers stay active after they leave

Manual offboarding across 20+ systems is slow and error-prone. Accounts belonging to departed employees remain active for days or weeks, creating serious security exposure.

Role changes accumulate excess access

When someone changes role, old entitlements are rarely cleaned up. Over time, users accumulate access far beyond what their current role requires, violating least-privilege principles.

Licence spend is invisible

Without a unified inventory, no one knows exactly how many licences are owned, allocated, or wasted. Renewals happen at full cost even when half the seats haven't been used in months.

Compliance relies on spreadsheets

Without a tamper-resistant audit trail, responding to an auditor's question about who had access to what — and when — takes days of manual investigation.

Assets disappear when people leave

Hardware recovery depends on emails and informal follow-up. When there is no tracked workflow, assets walk out the door or remain unaccounted for at audit time.

Capabilities

Three workstreams. One connected platform.

Each engine solves a distinct problem. Together they create a complete identity, licence, and asset management system.

Workstream A

Every hire, move, and departure is handled automatically.

The JML Lifecycle Engine connects directly to Workday. The moment a hire, role change, or termination event is detected, the engine orchestrates provisioning or deprovisioning across all connected systems. No manual steps, no delays, no gaps.

  • Access delta on every role change; prior entitlements removed, new ones granted automatically
  • Day 1 Ready: new hire accounts confirmed active before the start date
  • Tamper-resistant correlation log; every access change traced back to its Workday event
  • Separation of duties enforcement and break-glass emergency access with full audit trail
  • Same-day leaver deprovisioning across platforms
Workstream B

Stop paying for licences no one is using.

The Licence Management engine inventories every software seat across up to 20 platforms, reclaims dormant and orphaned licences automatically, and runs structured recertification campaigns. Savings are tracked and reportable from day one.

  • RBAC-driven assignment; licences granted automatically when a role is set in Workday
  • Full licence inventory: entitlements vs. allocations vs. active usage, refreshed nightly
  • Savings pipeline: identified, in-progress, and realised savings separated and reportable
  • Manager-led recertification: Accept or Decline per user, with campaign tracking and deadline alerts
  • Automated reclaim: leaver events and inactivity thresholds trigger instant seat recovery
Workstream C

Every asset tracked, from first assignment to final recovery.

The Asset Management engine maintains a single authoritative registry for all hardware and software assets, linked to identity and licence records. Nothing falls through the gap.

  • Joiner fulfilment: Workstream A hire events automatically generate equipment preparation tasks
  • Single asset registry: every in-scope asset with 10+ standard fields, linked to owner identity
  • Orphan asset detection: unowned equipment surfaced automatically for remediation
  • Leaver recovery: termination events create tracked recovery workflows for every assigned asset

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to evaluate this program.

How long does implementation take?+
Phased across three workstreams: Workstream A (JML) first, then B and C in parallel once the licence baseline is established. Timeline depends on platform count and policy complexity.
Which platforms and systems are supported?+
Up to 20 SaaS platforms with API-based provisioning. Core integrations include Microsoft Entra ID, Active Directory, Workday, and ServiceNow. Additional platforms connect via modular connectors.
What happens to our existing licence data?+
It's validated and brought into the platform as a verified baseline before automation goes live. Nothing is discarded; existing records are linked to identity data and used as the starting point for reclaim and recertification.
How are contractors and non-standard worker types handled?+
Contractors follow distinct workflow paths automatically. Worker type is derived from Workday attributes and drives the appropriate access, entitlements, and offboarding treatment — no manual routing needed.
What does a recertification campaign look like for a manager?+
A structured list of direct reports and their current licences. One decision per combination: Accept or Decline. Decisions are recorded immediately. Progress is tracked in real time with deadline reminders sent automatically.
How is the program handed over after delivery?+
Full documentation, runbooks, and connector configurations are delivered alongside a handover session. The platform is designed to run independently. No ongoing EVOCS involvement required after handoff.
Is the audit trail truly tamper-resistant?+
Yes. No user role (including administrators) can edit or delete audit records. Every change carries a correlation ID traceable to its Workday event. Attempted deletions are themselves logged as security events.
Does this replace our service desk or ITSM tool?+
No. It works alongside ServiceNow. Failed provisioning steps automatically create routed tickets with full context and close them when resolved. Your ITSM processes stay unchanged.

PIRO

From Reactive Reporting to Predictive Safety Intelligence.

Most data centre EHS teams operate in a reactive cycle: an incident happens, it gets logged, and the investigation begins after the fact. Historical data sits in disconnected systems without a unified way to surface patterns, predict future events, or prioritise where to allocate safety resources.

PIRO replaces that cycle with a predictive intelligence layer. It ingests incident records, near-miss reports, sensor streams, and workforce data into a unified Azure Data Lake, processes them through eight machine learning models, and delivers ranked corrective actions.

The problem

Safety Data Is Everywhere. Safety Intelligence Is Nowhere.

The challenges that keep EHS teams stuck in a reactive cycle — and the risks that come with it.

Incidents are investigated, never predicted

Your team responds to events after they happen. Historical incident data exists across multiple systems, but there is no mechanism to surface forward-looking risk signals or forecast where the next event is likely to occur.

Risk classification is manual and inconsistent

Classifying incidents into severity tiers depends on individual practitioner judgement. Without a standardised, data-driven classification engine, risk scoring varies from site to site and shift to shift.

Sensor anomalies are detected too late

Without real-time anomaly detection, unusual vibration patterns, temperature spikes, or equipment degradation signals go unnoticed until they contribute to an incident.

Near-miss narratives go unanalysed

Free-text incident descriptions and near-miss reports contain critical intelligence, but extracting structured insights from unstructured text at scale requires NLP capabilities most EHS teams do not have.

Resource allocation is based on intuition

Deciding which campus, shift, or team receives priority safety resources is driven by experience rather than optimised, data-driven allocation. High-risk sites may be under-resourced.

Safety data lives in silos

No single platform brings EHS sources together, making pattern recognition across systems nearly impossible. Every challenge compounds over time, and the cost is measured in incidents that could have been prevented.

Capabilities

Eight Models. One Integrated Safety Intelligence Pipeline.

Each module solves a specific EHS problem. Together, they form a connected pipeline that moves from raw data to ranked corrective actions.

NLP Intelligence

Extract Structured Insights from Every Incident Narrative.

Free-text incident descriptions, near-miss reports, and after-action reports are processed to extract severity labels, named entities, and sentiment scores. These structured outputs feed the risk classification and action recommendation models downstream.

  • Automated extraction: location, equipment type, affected body part, environmental conditions
  • Sentiment analysis flags critical safety culture signals across shifts and campuses
  • Processes After-Action Reports for post-incident intelligence capture
Risk Classification

Classify Every Event by Severity Automatically.

Each incident and near-miss is classified into severity tiers using multi-label classification. The platform shows practitioners exactly which factors drove each risk score, building trust and providing an auditable decision trail.

  • SHAP explainability artifacts attached to every prediction for audit and trust
  • Multi-label classification into four industry-standard severity tiers
  • Benchmarked against LightGBM and Random Forest — best performer selected per dataset
  • Feeds directly into Monte Carlo simulation and the action recommendation engine
Incident Forecasting

Forecast Incident Frequency 30, 60, and 90 Days Ahead.

A two-layer neural network trained on historical incidents, sensor data, training compliance, and external benchmarks produces forecasts with confidence intervals. Walk-forward validation ensures predictions are tested against real future data.

  • Lag features at 7, 14, and 30-day windows plus rolling 90-day averages
  • 30, 60, and 90-day forecast horizons per campus with confidence intervals
  • Walk-forward validation on 12-month rolling windows
Action Recommendations

Deliver Ranked Corrective Actions.

The terminal model in the critical path combines GPT generative intelligence with deterministic MCDA scoring to produce 5–10 ranked corrective actions per event.

  • RAG retrieval from historical data grounds recommendations in proven past actions
  • 5–10 ranked corrective actions per event, scored by effectiveness, feasibility, cost, time, and risk reduction
  • Operational from Week 1: quality improves progressively as real incident data is received
  • Direct ServiceNow integration via REST API
Resource Optimisation

Allocate Safety Resources Where They Matter Most.

A linear programming optimizer analyses feature importance rankings, historical allocation outcomes, and current risk profiles to recommend which campuses, shifts, and teams should receive priority safety resources.

  • Uses Random Forest feature importance rankings as state input features
  • Optimised resource allocation across campuses, shifts, and teams
  • Progressive ramp: accuracy improves as historical allocation data accumulates
  • 50,000 simulation runs using Latin Hypercube Sampling for scenario coverage
How it works

From Architecture Review to Predictive Operations in Phases.

A phased delivery model that proves value early and scales with your data maturity.

01

Data Readiness Assessment

Existing EHS data sources are catalogued, quality-assessed, and mapped to the ingestion schema. Gaps are identified and a data remediation plan is produced before any modelling begins.

02

Architecture Alignment & Azure Configuration

The Azure Data Lake, event ingestion pipeline, and compute environment are configured within your tenant. All components remain inside your security boundary — no data leaves your environment.

03

Model Training & Validation

As real incident data flows through the ingestion pipeline, models are trained and validated. Walk-forward validation confirms forecast accuracy, and anomaly detection is calibrated against live IoT sensor streams.

04

Dashboard & Integration Activation

The EHS Dashboard, ServiceNow integration, and alert routing are activated. Safety leaders gain immediate visibility into risk scores, forecasts, and ranked corrective actions from day one.

05

Hand Over and Sustain

The platform is handed over with full documentation, model refresh schedules, and trained operators. Accuracy improves progressively as more real incident data accumulates.
